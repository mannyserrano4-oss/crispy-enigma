Style Your New Cut

  • For Volume/Control: Start with damp, towel-dried hair. Apply a dime-sized amount of product and use a blow-dryer on low heat to shape.
  • For Curls/Texture: Apply a curl cream or leave-in conditioner to damp hair. Let it air-dry or use a blow-dryer with a diffuser attachment to prevent frizz.
  • Final Touch: Once dry, use your fingers or a comb for the final shape. A tiny bit of product can tame any flyaways.

Products I Recommend

Matte Styling Paste

My go-to for a textured, natural-looking hold with no shine. Great for straight to wavy hair.

Pro-Tip

Look for: Clay or Kaolin in the ingredients. These absorb oil and light, creating a true matte finish.
Avoid: Products with high amounts of drying alcohol (like 'Alcohol Denat.') which can make hair brittle.

Water-Based Pomade

Perfect for a slicker, high-shine look or for controlling thick, unruly hair. Provides a stronger hold.

Pro-Tip

Look for: Water-Based formulas. These give you a great hold but wash out easily with just water, preventing buildup.
Avoid: Heavy, petroleum-based pomades. They're hard to wash out and can clog pores on your scalp.

Moisturizing Curl Cream

Essential for defining curls and waves while reducing frizz. Won't make hair crunchy.

Pro-Tip

Look for: Sulfate-free and paraben-free formulas. Ingredients like Shea Butter or Aloe Vera are great for hydration.
Avoid: Heavy waxes or drying alcohols, which weigh curls down and cause frizz.

Leave-In Conditioner

A must-have for coily, kinky, or very dry hair. Provides daily moisture and protection.

Pro-Tip

Look for: Natural moisturizers like Jojoba Oil, Argan Oil, or Shea Butter. These are easily absorbed.
Avoid: Silicones (like 'dimethicone'). They create a plastic-like coating that feels smooth but blocks real moisture from getting in.

Natural Beard Oil

The most important product for any beard. It moisturizes the skin underneath to stop itch and flakes.

Pro-Tip

Look for: A "carrier" oil like Jojoba or Argan as the main ingredient. These are best for your skin.
Avoid: Products that just use "Fragrance" or "Parfum." These synthetic scents can irritate the sensitive skin on your face.

Make Your Cut Last

  • Fades & Tapers: A fade or taper looks sharpest for the first 5-7 days. To keep that clean look, schedule a touch-up or 'line-up' every 2 weeks.
  • Beards: Wash your beard 2-3 times a week, but apply beard oil daily to clean, damp skin to prevent dryness and itch.
  • Clean Neckline: Carefully shave the stray hairs on the back of your neck between cuts to keep your lines looking fresh.

Healthy Hair Guide

For Straight to Wavy Hair

Your main challenge is managing oil. Don't over-wash, as this can make your scalp produce more oil. A light conditioner on the ends only is often all you need.

For Curly Hair

Moisture is key. Avoid shampoos with sulfates, which are very drying. Consider "co-washing" (washing with conditioner only). Never brush dry curlsâ€”this causes breakage and frizz. Use a wide-tooth comb in the shower.

For Coily / Kinky Hair (Type 4)

Your hair needs heavy-duty moisture and protection. Use rich leave-in conditioners, butters, and oils. Shampoo sparingly (e.g., once every 1-2 weeks). Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase (or use a durag) to minimize breakage and retain moisture.
